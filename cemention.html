Modified Cementation

This article is aimed to demonstrate an almost costless solution to the problem of potential rock fracture when cementing J-, S-trajectory or ERD well, taking advantage of their trajectory feature, without the use of technologies that increase the technical complexity or costs of well a construction.

The idea is to use a existing well's trajectory (J-type or S-type) to reduce the equivalent circulation density (ECD) exerted on the formations during liner cementing. This is achieved by modifying the sequence of pumped fluids during cementing operation.

A portion of drilling mud with a normal density is replaced with a portion of lighter mud.

The position of this portion of the lighter mud in the pumping sequence is chosen so that at the moment when cement plug bumps (end of cement displacement) this portion of the lighter mud is placed just under wellhead at the well vertical upper section in the annulus between the drill pipes and the production casing.

Well control is top priority, therefore combination of volume and density of light drilling mud must ensure that the minimum equivalent fluid gradient (yellow line ) in the well exceeds the pore pressure gradient (green line ) at any time of cementing.

Another requirement is stability of the open hole walls, in order to prevent the possibility of formation collapse, it is necessary to maintain the minimum equivalent fluid gradient (yellow line ) in the open hole above the formation stability gradient (black line ).

Modified pumping sequence reduces bottom hole pressure. ECD of conventional cementation (blue line ) shifts under formation strength gradient (red line ) to a lower value (yellow line with hollow dots ), so that risk of formation fracture and losses is eliminated.

The modified cemention is economical with a significant effect. Lightweight mud can be reused for drilling. Autocirculation is enhanced by a reinforced siphon. The final displacement pressure on the pumps is lower than in a conventional cementing.
